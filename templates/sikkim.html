Sikkim

Sikkim, nestled in the northeastern part of India, is a picturesque state bordered by Bhutan, Nepal, and Tibet. It's renowned for its stunning landscapes, including the majestic Himalayas, lush green valleys, and diverse flora and fauna. Sikkim is also known for its rich cultural heritage, colorful festivals, and warm hospitality.

Attractions:
Tsomgo Lake (Changu Lake)
Nathu La Pass
Rumtek Monastery
Yumthang Valley
Gurudongmar Lake
Pelling
Namchi
Ravangla
Zuluk


Tips:
-Obtain Inner Line Permit (ILP) for visiting restricted areas like Nathu La Pass.
-Respect local customs and traditions, especially in monasteries and sacred sites.
-Dress in layers as the weather can be unpredictable, especially in higher altitudes.
-Carry essential medications, especially if you're prone to altitude sickness.
-Plan your itinerary well in advance, considering travel time between attractions.
-Hire local guides for a deeper understanding of the culture and history.
-Taste the local cuisine, including momos, thukpa, and traditional Sikkimese dishes.

The nearest major railway station is New Jalpaiguri (NJP) Railway Station, also in West Bengal.
Bus:
Regular bus services connect Kasol with major cities like Delhi and Chandigarh.
Road:
Sikkim is well-connected by road to neighboring states like West Bengal and Assam.
